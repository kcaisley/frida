\vspace{4pt}
\noindent\textbf{Notes (Table 1):}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
  \item \textbf{FE limit} = front-end pile-up limit, approximated as $1/\tau_\mathrm{dead}$ for a non-paralyzable discriminator.
  \item \textbf{BE limit} = back-end readout limit $=$ counter full-scale $\times$ max frame rate (or data link bandwidth).
  \item \textbf{Neck} = which limit is tighter: FE (pile-up) or BE (readout bandwidth).
  \item ``Reported'' values are taken directly from the cited paper and may include detailed dead-time correction.
  \item For Timepix4, photon-counting (PC) mode is shown; it yields a higher imaging rate than
    data-driven (DD) mode, which is bandwidth-limited to ${\sim}1.1\times10^4$\,hits/px/s.
  \item Max rate/cm$^2$ = (max rate/px) $/$ (pitch in cm)$^2$.  n/r = not reported.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Notes (Table 2):}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
  \item \textbf{Well depth} = analog integration capacity per pixel per frame (primary electrons at stated beam energy), \emph{before} any charge-removal extension unless noted otherwise.
  \item \textbf{Max rate/px/s} $=$ well depth $\times$ max frame rate.
  \item \textbf{Max rate/cm$^2$/s} $=$ (max rate/px/s) $/$ (pitch in cm)$^2$.
  \item For EMPAD/EMPAD-G2, the per-frame capacity includes in-frame charge removal; the single-integration analog well is smaller but not separately reported.
  \item For Sagara1212, well depth is approximated from the ADC full-scale ($2^{10}$); the actual analog well may differ.
  \item[$\dagger$] Caeleste/Photron and Phantom TMX 7510 are \textbf{visible-light} imager designs, not electron detectors.
    Well depths are in photoelectrons (1 photon $\approx$ 1\,e$^-$).
    For high-energy primary electrons (${\sim}$100--300\,keV), each hit deposits hundreds to thousands of signal electrons,
    so the effective well depth in primary electrons per frame, and hence the max hit rate, would be much lower.
    These sensors have not been characterised for direct electron detection.
  \item The 4D Camera and K2 operate in a sparse-counting regime (${\sim}$1\,e$^-$/px/frame); the well depth reflects the counting-mode operating point, not the full analog capacity.
  \item n/r = not reported.
\end{itemize}
